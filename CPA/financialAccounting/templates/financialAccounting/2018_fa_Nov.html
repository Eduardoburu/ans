(b) The income statement of United Trading Enterprises (UTE) for the year ended 31 December 2017, as prepared by an accounts assistant, indicated a net profit of Sh. 49,360,000. The cash book as at 31 December 2017 showed a balance at bank of Sh. 6,440,000. Your attention, however, has been drawn to the following matters:

1. Cheques from customers totaling Sh. 4,980,000 which were recorded in the cash book on 29th December 2017 were actually not credited by the bank until 2nd January 2018.

2. Cheques issued on 31st December 2017 totaling Sh. 7,420,000 in favour of suppliers were not paid by the bank until after the end of the year (that is, after 31 December 2017).

3. On 22nd November 2017, the bank paid an amount of sh. 3,600,000 with respect to a standing order from UTE for rent of business premises for the three months to 31st January 2018 but, unfortunately, no entry for the payment had been made in the cash book. Additionally, no record of this rent had been made in the income statement for the period.

4. On 31st December 2017, Mr. Ken Abusi, a customer, had paid sh. 2,340,000 directly into UTE’s bank account in full settlement of a debt of Sh. 2,400,000 owing to UTE but no entry had been made in the books.

5. On 31st December 2017, a cheque of sh. 480,000 was received from a customer in the settlement of sales invoice for the same amount. The cheques were lodged into UTE’s bank account. However, still, on 31st December 2017, the customer returned the goods and also instructed his bankers not to pay the cheque. This instruction was carried out the same day but no entries in respect of these latter developments have been made in UTE’s books. The cost of these goods amounting to sh. 320,000 was not included in the closing inventories.

6. A cheque of sh. 840,000 from an insurance company in the settlement of a claim for damage to inventory by fire had been paid into and credited by the bank on 21st December 2017, but an estimated amount of sh. 800,000 had been entered in UTE’s income statement.

Required

a) Adjusted cash book as at 31st December 2017 (4 marks)

b) Bank reconciliation statement as at 31st December (4 marks)

c) Statement of corrected net profit for the year ended 31st December 2017 (6 marks)

d) Explain how the bank balance will be reported in UTE’s final accounts. (2 marks)
